# Your Mission at Precision Manufacturing Co.

---

## Welcome to Your New Role

Congratulations on your hire as **AI Operations Lead** at Precision Manufacturing Co.!

The CEO, Sarah Chen, brought you in after reading about "AI automating everything" in the business press. She knows the company is drowning in manual processes but isn't sure where to start.

Your interview was... interesting:

> **Sarah:** "Look, I'll be honest. We're a 50-year-old manufacturing company that still runs half our operations like it's 1990. Excel spreadsheets everywhere. People spending entire days copying data from one system to another. I hired you because I need someone who understands both the AI stuff AND business operations. Can you help?"

You said yes. Now you're here.

## Your Mission (3 Months)

Sarah gave you a clear mandate:

### Phase 1: Investigation (Weeks 1-2)
- Talk to department heads
- Understand current pain points
- Identify quick-win opportunities
- Create automation roadmap

### Phase 2: Quick Wins (Weeks 3-8)
- Build 3-5 working automations
- Focus on high-impact, low-complexity
- Document everything
- Train users

### Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 9-12)
- Expand successful automations
- Create repeatable templates
- Build team capability
- Prepare sustainability plan

**Success Metric:** Save 100+ hours per month across departments

## The Real Challenge

It's not the technology.

It's not even the code.

The real challenge is:
1. **Prioritization** - 5 departments, all in pain, you can't help everyone at once
2. **Adoption** - Building something nobody uses is worthless
3. **Sustainability** - You won't be there forever, it needs to last
4. **Expectations** - Sarah thinks AI will solve everything overnight
5. **Politics** - Some people are scared of automation

You'll need to:
- Choose the right first projects
- Build trust through quick wins
- Document obsessively
- Train, not just build
- Manage up (keep Sarah realistic)
