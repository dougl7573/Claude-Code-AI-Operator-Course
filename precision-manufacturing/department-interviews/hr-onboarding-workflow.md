# HR Onboarding Interview - Rachel Kim

**Date:** Week 4 of your new role
**Department:** Human Resources
**Team Lead:** Rachel Kim
**Team Size:** 2 people (Rachel + HR Coordinator, Angela)

---

## The Conversation

You met Rachel in the small HR office. Her desk was covered in paper - literally stacks of forms.

**You:** "Rachel! Thanks for meeting. Sarah mentioned you're dealing with some manual onboarding processes?"

**Rachel:** (organizing a stack of papers) "Manual onboarding? That's putting it mildly. We're basically running a 1990s HR department with 2025 hiring volumes. Grab a seat - let me show you the chaos."

*She pulls out a binder labeled 'New Hire Onboarding Checklist - 2025'*

**Rachel:** "This... is our onboarding process.

For EVERY new hire, Angela or I have to:

**Week Before Start Date:**
1. Fill out offer letter template (Word doc from 2015)
2. Customize: name, position, salary, start date, manager
3. Print, sign, scan, email
4. Create employee file folder (physical + digital)
5. Fill out 8 HR forms:
   - Emergency contact form
   - Direct deposit form
   - W-4 tax form
   - I-9 employment verification
   - Benefits election form
   - Equipment request form
   - IT access request
   - Parking/badge request
6. Email IT department (manually)
7. Email facilities for desk setup
8. Order computer + phone (manual form to IT)
9. Create onboarding schedule
10. Email manager with 'new hire prep' checklist

**First Day:**
1. Meet new hire at 9 AM
2. Walk through all the forms they need to sign (most are duplicates of what we sent)
3. Give them employee handbook (200-page PDF, printed)
4. IT setup (1 hour of password resets and logins)
5. Benefits presentation (1 hour)
6. Office tour
7. Introduce to team
8. Hope we didn't forget anything

**First Week:**
1. Follow-up emails (How's it going? Do you have questions?)
2. Check that IT actually gave them access to everything
3. Nag manager to schedule 1-on-1s

**Total Time Per New Hire:** About 3 hours of pure administrative work.

**Frequency:** We're hiring 8-10 people per month right now (company is growing).

**That's 24-30 hours per month. Just. Filling. Out. Forms."

**You:** "That's basically a full-time job."

**Rachel:** "YUP. And it's the SAME forms, SAME emails, SAME checklist every single time.

I have a master's degree in HR. I should be focused on recruiting strategy, culture initiatives, performance management.

Instead, I'm copying and pasting names into Word templates from 2015."

---

## The Pain Points

**You:** "What's the most frustrating part?"

**Rachel:** "Let me count the ways:

**1. It's Incredibly Repetitive**
Every hire is the SAME process. Same forms. Same emails. Same checklist.

I literally have a 'New Hire Email Templates' document with 12 templates:
- 'Welcome to the team!'
- 'IT access request'
- 'Facilities setup request'
- 'Manager onboarding prep'
- 'First day agenda'
- Etc.

I copy-paste from this doc 50+ times per hire.

**2. Error-Prone**
When you're filling out the same form for the 100th time, you make mistakes:
- Wrong start date
- Misspelled name
- Wrong manager
- Wrong salary (scary!)

Last month, I sent an offer letter with the wrong salary ($75K instead of $70K). Caught it before the candidate signed, but... yikes.

**3. Things Fall Through the Cracks**
Example: New hire starts on Monday. Friday afternoon I realize... I forgot to email IT.

They show up on Monday. No computer. No logins. They sit in the conference room for 2 hours while IT scrambles.

So embarrassing. Bad first impression. Entirely preventable.

**4. No Visibility for Managers**
Managers have NO IDEA where we are in the onboarding process.

They email me: 'When does Sarah start? Will she have a laptop?'

I have to check my email threads to remember what I've done.

There's no dashboard, no tracking, no visibility. It's all in my head (or Angela's).

**5. Paper Everywhere**
We have physical employee files (legally required for some forms). But we also have digital files (Google Drive).

They're not always in sync. Sometimes the paper file has a signed form but it's not scanned into Drive. Sometimes Drive has the form but paper file doesn't.

It's chaos.

**6. Can't Scale**
Right now, 8-10 hires per month is manageable (barely). But Sarah wants to grow to 15-20 hires per month next year.

At that volume? We'd need to hire another HR person JUST for onboarding admin.

Or... we automate it."

---

## What They've Tried

**You:** "Have you tried to streamline this?"

**Rachel:** "Oh yes. Multiple times.

**Attempt 1: Update to Google Docs Templates**
We migrated from Word to Google Docs (modern! cloud-based!).

Thought maybe Google Docs would be easier to customize with mail merge or something.

Result:
- Still manual
- Formatting breaks when you copy-paste
- No real improvement over Word
- Gave up after 2 months

**Attempt 2: BambooHR Trial (Real HR Software)**
I convinced Sarah to trial BambooHR for 3 months.

It was AMAZING:
- Digital onboarding workflows
- Automated emails
- Document signing (DocuSign integration)
- Tracking dashboard

But...
- Cost: $150/month for our company size
- Setup: 40 hours to configure everything
- Integration: Didn't integrate with our payroll system (ADP)
- Sarah said: 'Too expensive for what we need'

Canceled after trial. Back to manual.

**Attempt 3: Zapier Workflows**
Angela tried to set up Zapier automation:
- New hire enters into Google Sheet
- Triggers automated emails
- Creates folder in Google Drive

Problems:
- Google Sheets as a 'database' is clunky
- Zapier only handles simple logic (can't do conditional workflows)
- Cost: $20/month (manageable) but didn't save enough time
- Broke when we changed Sheet structure

Used it for 6 weeks. Abandoned it.

**Current State:** Back to manual Word docs, manual emails, manual everything."

---

## What Success Would Look Like

**You:** "If you could design the perfect onboarding system - budget permitting - what would it do?"

**Rachel:** "Okay, dream scenario:

**Ideal Onboarding Workflow:**

**Step 1: Trigger (New Hire Accepted Offer)**
- I enter new hire info into a simple form (name, position, start date, salary, manager)
- System stores this in a database or spreadsheet

**Step 2: Document Generation (Automated)**
- System auto-generates:
  - Offer letter (with correct name, position, salary, start date)
  - Welcome email
  - First-day agenda
- Uses templates I've created, but fills in the details automatically
- Sends to me for review before sending to candidate

**Step 3: Task Automation (Automated Emails)**
- System sends:
  - Email to IT: 'New hire starting [date], please set up laptop and accounts'
  - Email to facilities: 'New desk needed in [department]'
  - Email to manager: 'Your new hire starts [date], here's your prep checklist'
- All emails are triggered automatically based on timeline (e.g., 1 week before start date)

**Step 4: Tracking Dashboard (Visibility)**
- I can see status of all new hires:
  - 'Offer sent, waiting for signature'
  - 'IT setup in progress'
  - 'First day scheduled'
- Managers can see it too (so they stop emailing me)

**Step 5: First Day Workflow**
- New hire receives welcome email with:
  - First-day agenda
  - Links to forms (digital, not paper)
  - Employee handbook (PDF)
- They fill out forms online (emergency contact, direct deposit, etc.)
- Data flows into our records automatically (no re-typing)

**What This Gives Us:**
- My 3 hours per hire → 20 minutes (just reviewing and approving)
- Fewer errors (computer doesn't typo)
- Nothing falls through cracks (automated reminders)
- Managers have visibility
- Better new hire experience (professional, organized)
- Scales easily (20 hires/month? No problem)

**You:** "What about integration with existing systems? Payroll, benefits, etc.?"

**Rachel:** "Honestly? I'll settle for 'better than manual Word docs' first.

Integration with ADP payroll would be AMAZING, but that's probably complex.

For now, I just want:
- Document generation (offer letters, forms)
- Email automation (stop copy-pasting)
- Basic tracking (who's onboarding, what's done, what's pending)

If we nail that? I'll call it a huge win."
